1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your computer or mobile device when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently and provide information to website owners.

1.1 Types of Cookies

  • Session Cookies: Temporary cookies that expire when you close your browser
  • Persistent Cookies: Remain on your device for a set period or until you delete them
  • First-Party Cookies: Set by the website you're visiting
  • Third-Party Cookies: Set by external services (e.g., analytics, advertising platforms)

6. Your Choices and Control

6.1 Browser Settings

Most browsers allow you to control cookies through settings. You can:

  • Block all cookies
  • Block third-party cookies only
  • Delete cookies after each session
  • Accept cookies from specific sites

Browser Cookie Settings:

  • Google Chrome
  • Mozilla Firefox
  • Safari
  • Microsoft Edge

6.2 Opt-Out of Advertising

You can opt out of interest-based advertising:

  • Your Online Choices (Europe)
  • Network Advertising Initiative
  • Digital Advertising Alliance
  • Google Ads Settings
  • Facebook Ad Preferences

6.3 Mobile Device Settings

On mobile devices, you can limit ad tracking:

  • iOS: Settings → Privacy → Advertising → Limit Ad Tracking
  • Android: Settings → Google → Ads → Opt out of Ads Personalization

6.4 Do Not Track

Some browsers have "Do Not Track" features. Currently, there is no industry standard for responding to these signals, and we do not respond to Do Not Track requests at this time.
